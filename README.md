Executive Summary:
This project demonstrates an end-to-end Business Intelligence solution developed to analyze a residential real estate portfolio. Using Excel, SQL and Power BI, the solution identifies revenue optimization opportunities, evaluates collection risk, measures maintenance efficiency, and supports investment-oriented decision making.

Objectives:
    •	Identify revenue optimization opportunities. 
    •	Evaluate portfolio occupancy and operational efficiency. 
    •	Assess receivables and collection risk. 
    •	Deliver executive dashboards to support asset management decisions.

Business Problem:
Residential portfolios contain unrealized income potential due to below-market rents, long-term tenancies, regulatory constraints, and operational inefficiencies, while at the same time, portfolio managers must balance revenue growth with tenant risk, maintenance costs, and regulatory compliance.

The project was designed to answer such business questions as:
    •	How much annual revenue is currently generated by the portfolio?
    •	Where is the greatest embedded revenue opportunity?
    •	How efficiently are rents being captured relative to legal rent levels?
    •	Which tenants represent the highest credit exposure?
    •	How does property quality influence rental income?
    •	What financial impact do renovation opportunities create?
    •	Which units should be prioritized for revenue optimization?

Dataset:
The analytical model integrates operational, financial, maintenance, and tenancy data to support portfolio-level reporting
The project uses an anonymized residential portfolio dataset containing:
    •	Unit information
    •	Current and legal rents
    •	Tenant tenure
    •	Property type
    •	Income segmentation
    •	Maintenance costs
    •	Payment history
    •	Amenity information
    •	Arrears balances

All personal and confidential information has been removed.

SQL Techniques:

•	INNER JOIN 
Combined related portfolio tables to retrieve matching records across units, tenants, and financial data.
    
•	LEFT JOIN 
Preserved all property records while incorporating optional information such as maintenance history or receivables, even when related records were unavailable.
    
•	Common Table Expressions (CTEs) 
Used to simplify multi-step revenue calculations.
    
•	CASE statements 
Classified units into business categories such as regulated versus free-market properties, aging buckets, and occupancy status.
    
•	Aggregate functions (SUM, AVG, COUNT, MAX)
Calculated portfolio KPIs including annual revenue, average repair cost, total arrears, occupancy, and maintenance burden.
    
•	GROUP BY 
Summarized portfolio performance by building, regulation status, property type, and individual units.
    
•	Window Functions (if you used them) 
Applied to rank revenue opportunities and receivables.
    
•	Views
Created reusable analytical datasets for Power BI.

SQL replaced the Excel preparation layer by automating data cleaning, integrating multiple portfolio datasets, and generating reusable reporting views that support both the Excel dashboard and the Power BI report.

Business Impact
    •	Identified $79K in annual revenue optimization opportunities. 
    •	Quantified portfolio revenue growth potential at 3.56%. 
    •	Maintained a high portfolio occupancy rate of 98.85%. 
    •	Confirmed a low portfolio maintenance burden of 1.01% of annual rental revenue.
    •	Measured revenue at risk at 6.07% of annual rental revenue. 
    •	Revealed that 94% of outstanding receivables are concentrated within regulated units. 
    •	Identified a significant concentration risk, with one tenant accounting for 30% of total receivables.
